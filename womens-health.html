Women's Health Physiotherapy in Dubai

Specialized physiotherapy for women at every stage of life. Our experienced women's health team provides compassionate care for pelvic floor dysfunction, prenatal and postnatal conditions, and chronic pelvic pain.

Women's Health Physiotherapy

Women's health physiotherapy is a specialized field that addresses conditions unique to women or those that disproportionately affect women. From pregnancy and postpartum recovery to pelvic floor dysfunction and chronic pelvic pain, our team provides evidence-based, compassionate care in a comfortable and respectful environment.

At Tadawi Clinic, our female physiotherapists understand the sensitive nature of these conditions and are dedicated to helping women regain function, reduce pain, and improve their quality of life.

Conditions We Treat

Pelvic Floor Disorders

  • Stress Urinary Incontinence: Leakage during coughing, sneezing, laughing, or physical activity due to pelvic floor weakness
  • Urge Incontinence and Overactive Bladder: Sudden, intense urge to urinate with or without leakage
  • Pelvic Organ Prolapse: Sensation of heaviness or dragging due to descent of bladder, uterus, or rectum
  • Diastasis Recti: Separation of the abdominal muscles during and after pregnancy
  • Pelvic Pain: Chronic or recurrent pain in the pelvic region, including dyspareunia (painful intercourse)
  • Pre and Post-Surgical Rehabilitation: Recovery from hysterectomy, prolapse repair, and other pelvic surgeries

Pregnancy-Related Conditions

  • Pregnancy-Related Low Back Pain: Lumbar and pelvic girdle pain during pregnancy
  • Sacroiliac Joint Dysfunction: Pelvic joint instability during pregnancy
  • Symphysis Pubis Dysfunction: Pain at the front of the pelvis due to pubic joint dysfunction
  • Pregnancy-Related Carpal Tunnel Syndrome: Nerve compression in the wrist during pregnancy
  • Preparation for Labor and Delivery: Pelvic floor education, positioning, and perineal massage guidance

Postnatal Recovery

  • Postnatal Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation: Restoring pelvic floor strength and function after delivery
  • Postnatal Abdominal Recovery: Addressing diastasis recti and core weakness
  • C-Section Recovery: Scar management, core reactivation, and progressive return to activity
  • Vaginal Delivery Recovery: Perineal healing, pelvic floor restoration, and return to exercise
  • Return to Exercise: Safe, progressive guidance for returning to fitness activities after childbirth

Other Women's Health Conditions

  • Endometriosis-Related Pain: Physiotherapy management of pelvic pain associated with endometriosis
  • Menopause-Related Musculoskeletal Changes: Bone density, joint stiffness, and pelvic floor considerations
  • Breast Cancer Rehabilitation: Upper limb mobility, scar management, and lymphedema prevention post-mastectomy
  • Osteoporosis Prevention and Management: Exercise programs for bone health and fall prevention

Our Assessment Process

Our women's health assessments are conducted in a private, comfortable setting:

  • Detailed History: Comprehensive discussion of symptoms, medical history, pregnancy/birth history, and goals
  • Postural Assessment: Evaluating spinal alignment, pelvic position, and muscle balance
  • Abdominal Assessment: Checking for diastasis recti, core muscle activation, and scar tissue
  • Pelvic Floor Assessment: Internal and/or external assessment of pelvic floor muscle strength, tone, and coordination (with consent)
  • Musculoskeletal Screening: Hip, pelvis, and spine mobility and strength testing
  • Functional Assessment: Evaluation of impact on daily activities, exercise, and quality of life

Our Treatment Approach

Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation

  • Pelvic Floor Muscle Training (PFMT): Evidence-based Kegel exercise programs with biofeedback guidance
  • Electrical Stimulation: For cases where voluntary contraction is difficult to achieve
  • Coordination Training: Teaching proper timing of pelvic floor contraction during functional activities
  • Relaxation Techniques: For hypertonic pelvic floor dysfunction

Manual Therapy

  • Soft Tissue Mobilization: Release of tight muscles in the pelvis, hips, and abdominal wall
  • Scar Massage: C-section and perineal scar mobilization to improve tissue mobility
  • Joint Mobilization: Gentle techniques for sacroiliac and lumbar spine dysfunction
  • Myofascial Release: Addressing fascial restrictions in the pelvic region

Therapeutic Exercise

  • Core Stabilization: Progressive deep core and pelvic floor integration
  • Gluteal Strengthening: Hip and pelvic stability exercises
  • Prenatal Exercise Programs: Safe, modified exercise throughout pregnancy
  • Postnatal Exercise Progression: Graduated return to fitness activities
  • Pelvic Girdle Stabilization: Specific exercises for pregnancy-related pelvic instability

Patient Education

  • Bladder and Bowel Habits: Optimization of voiding patterns and toilet posture
  • Lifting and Breathing Mechanics: Safe strategies for daily activities
  • Pessary Education: Guidance on pessary use when appropriate
  • Lifestyle Modification: Weight management, fluid intake, and caffeine guidance

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I start pelvic floor exercises after pregnancy?

+

You can begin gentle pelvic floor exercises within days of delivery, as long as you feel comfortable. For vaginal deliveries, we recommend starting once any perineal pain subsides. For C-sections, we wait until the surgical wound is healing well. Our physiotherapists will guide you on the appropriate time to begin and progress your exercises safely.

Is pelvic floor physiotherapy painful?

+

Pelvic floor physiotherapy should not be painful. Internal assessments and treatments are gentle and performed only with your consent. If you experience any discomfort, your therapist will adjust the treatment immediately. Many women find pelvic floor therapy very effective and comfortable once they understand the process.

Can physiotherapy help with prolapse without surgery?

+

Yes, pelvic floor rehabilitation can significantly improve symptoms of mild-to-moderate pelvic organ prolapse. Studies show that consistent PFMT can reduce prolapse symptoms by 50-70% and may prevent progression. For severe prolapse, physiotherapy can still provide significant symptom relief and is often recommended alongside or instead of surgical options.

How long does postnatal recovery take?

+

Full postnatal recovery typically takes 6-12 months, though many women feel significantly better within 6-8 weeks with appropriate rehabilitation. The timeline depends on the type of delivery, pre-pregnancy fitness level, and adherence to rehabilitation. Our physiotherapists provide a structured, progressive program tailored to your recovery needs.
