Understanding Back Pain

What Is Back Pain and Why Does It Occur?

Back pain is one of the most common musculoskeletal complaints worldwide, affecting approximately 80% of adults at some point in their lives. It can range from a dull, constant ache to a sudden, sharp sensation that leaves you immobilized. While back pain can develop anywhere along the spine, it most frequently occurs in the lumbar region (lower back) due to the significant mechanical stress placed on the lower vertebrae during daily activities.

The spine is a complex structure consisting of 33 vertebrae, intervertebral discs, ligaments, muscles, and the spinal cord. When any of these components become inflamed, injured, or degenerate, pain signals are transmitted to the brain. Understanding the underlying cause of your back pain is the first step toward effective treatment and long-term recovery.

Types of Back Pain

Acute Back Pain

Sudden onset lasting less than 6 weeks, often caused by muscle strain, ligament sprain, or a specific injury. Acute back pain typically responds well to early intervention and targeted physical therapy.

Chronic Back Pain

Persistent pain lasting 12 weeks or longer, which may be constant or intermittent. Chronic back pain often involves complex factors including structural changes, nerve sensitization, and psychosocial components that require a multimodal treatment approach.

Radiating / Radicular Pain

Pain that travels from the spine into the buttocks, legs, or feet, often caused by nerve compression or irritation. Sciatica is the most common form of radicular pain, presenting as shooting pain, numbness, or tingling along the sciatic nerve pathway.

Common Causes of Back Pain

  • Muscle or ligament strain
  • Herniated or bulging discs
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Poor posture and ergonomics
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Facet joint syndrome
  • Scoliosis and spinal deformities
  • Sedentary lifestyle and weak core

Key Symptoms to Watch For

Back pain manifests differently depending on the underlying cause. Common symptoms include localized aching or stiffness, sharp or shooting pain with movement, muscle spasms, limited range of motion, and pain that radiates into the buttocks or legs. In some cases, patients may also experience numbness, tingling, or weakness in the extremities, which may indicate nerve involvement and requires prompt evaluation.

Seek Immediate Medical Attention If:

You experience loss of bladder or bowel control, progressive leg weakness, numbness in the groin or saddle area, or severe pain following trauma. These may indicate cauda equina syndrome or other serious conditions requiring emergency care.

Our Treatment Approach

Evidence-Based Back Pain Treatment

At Tadawi Clinic, we employ a comprehensive, multimodal approach to back pain treatment. Our therapists are trained in the latest evidence-based techniques and combine multiple modalities to address pain from every angle — reducing symptoms, correcting underlying dysfunction, and building resilience to prevent recurrence.

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques including spinal mobilization, manipulation, and soft tissue mobilization to restore joint mechanics, release muscle tension, reduce pain, and improve range of motion. Our therapists use graded techniques tailored to your pain sensitivity and condition severity.

Exercise Therapy

Customized exercise programs targeting core stabilization, spinal mobility, flexibility, and progressive strengthening. Exercise therapy is the cornerstone of long-term back pain recovery, addressing muscle imbalances and building functional strength to support the spine.

Dry Needling

Precise insertion of fine filament needles into myofascial trigger points within tight or dysfunctional muscles. Dry needling effectively releases muscle knots, reduces local inflammation, decreases pain, and restores normal muscle activation patterns — particularly beneficial for chronic paraspinal muscle tension.

Shockwave Therapy

Extracorporeal shockwave therapy (ESWT) delivers high-energy acoustic waves to targeted tissue areas, stimulating the body's natural healing response. Particularly effective for chronic tendinopathies, calcific deposits, and stubborn soft tissue conditions that have not responded to conventional treatment.

Posture Correction

Comprehensive postural assessment and retraining to identify and correct dysfunctional movement patterns that contribute to ongoing back pain. We use visual biofeedback, mirror training, and progressive motor control exercises to establish optimal spinal alignment during daily activities.

Ergonomic Advice

Practical guidance on workplace setup, sleeping positions, lifting techniques, and daily movement habits. Our therapists provide personalized recommendations for your home and work environment to minimize spinal stress and prevent the recurrence of symptoms.

Conditions We Treat

Back Pain Conditions We Treat

Our team has extensive experience treating a wide range of spinal and musculoskeletal conditions. Whether your pain is the result of an acute injury, chronic degeneration, or postural dysfunction, we have the expertise to develop an effective treatment plan.

Lower Back Pain

The most common form of back pain, affecting the lumbar spine and surrounding muscles. Often caused by muscle strain, poor posture, or degenerative changes in the lower vertebrae.

Upper Back Pain

Pain in the thoracic region, frequently associated with prolonged sitting, kyphotic posture, and muscular imbalances between the chest and upper back muscles.

Sciatica

Radiating pain along the sciatic nerve pathway from the lower back through the buttocks and leg. Often caused by disc herniation, piriformis syndrome, or spinal stenosis compressing the nerve root.

Disc Herniation

When the soft inner material of an intervertebral disc protrudes through its outer ring, potentially pressing on nearby nerves. Can cause localized pain, radicular symptoms, and neurological deficits.

Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal that puts pressure on the spinal cord and nerves. Common in older adults, causing pain, numbness, and weakness that typically worsens with standing or walking.

Muscle Strain

Overstretching or tearing of the muscles and ligaments of the back, usually from sudden movements, heavy lifting, or improper bending. Typically presents with localized pain and muscle spasm.

Facet Joint Syndrome

Degeneration or inflammation of the facet joints that connect the vertebrae. Causes localized pain that may radiate into the buttocks or thighs, often worsened by extension and rotation movements.

Scoliosis

Abnormal lateral curvature of the spine that can cause uneven muscle loading, postural imbalance, and chronic back pain. Our treatment focuses on curve management, muscle re-education, and pain relief.

Treatment Process

Your Back Pain Recovery Journey

Our structured treatment process ensures that every aspect of your condition is thoroughly assessed and addressed. From your first visit to full recovery, we guide you through each stage with personalized care.

Step 01

Comprehensive Assessment

Your initial consultation includes a detailed medical history review, physical examination of spinal mobility and posture, neurological screening, orthopedic testing, and functional movement analysis to identify the root cause of your pain.

Step 02

Accurate Diagnosis

Using findings from the assessment and any available imaging results, your therapist provides a clear diagnosis with explanation of the condition, contributing factors, and expected recovery timeline.

Step 03

Personalized Treatment Plan

A customized treatment strategy combining the most effective modalities for your specific condition. The plan includes short-term pain relief goals, mid-term functional restoration, and long-term prevention strategies.

Step 04

Active Treatment Phase

Regular therapy sessions combining hands-on manual therapy, targeted exercises, dry needling or shockwave therapy as needed, and progressive rehabilitation to restore mobility, strength, and function.

Step 05

Progress Monitoring

Continuous reassessment of your condition with objective outcome measures. Treatment plans are dynamically adjusted based on your response to ensure optimal results and prevent plateaus.

Step 06

Long-Term Prevention

Transition to a maintenance program with a home exercise routine, ergonomic strategies, and lifestyle modifications designed to prevent recurrence and maintain spinal health for life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Back Pain FAQ

How long does it take to recover from back pain with physical therapy?

Recovery time varies depending on the severity and cause of the condition. Acute back pain typically improves within 4-6 weeks with consistent therapy. Chronic conditions may require 8-12 weeks or longer. Most patients notice significant improvement within the first 2-3 weeks of treatment.

Do I need a referral to see a physical therapist for back pain?

No, you do not need a doctor's referral to book an appointment at Tadawi Clinic. You can schedule directly with us. However, if you have a referral or recent imaging results, please bring them along as they help us provide more accurate diagnosis and treatment.

Is physical therapy painful for back conditions?

Physical therapy should not cause excessive pain. While you may experience mild discomfort during certain exercises or manual techniques, your therapist will always work within your pain tolerance. The goal is to reduce pain over time, not increase it. Communicate openly with your therapist about your comfort level.

Can physical therapy help avoid back surgery?

Yes, research shows that conservative physical therapy management is effective for the majority of back pain cases and can help many patients avoid surgery. Conditions like disc herniation, spinal stenosis, and facet joint syndrome often respond well to targeted physical therapy. In cases where surgery is necessary, pre-surgical rehabilitation can improve post-operative outcomes.

How many sessions will I need for back pain treatment?

The number of sessions depends on your specific condition and goals. Most patients benefit from 2-3 sessions per week for 4-8 weeks. During your initial assessment, your therapist will create a personalized treatment plan with a projected timeline. We continuously reassess your progress and adjust the plan accordingly.
