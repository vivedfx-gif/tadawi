Pediatric Physical Therapy in Dubai

Play-based, child-centered physiotherapy to help infants, children, and adolescents reach their full developmental potential. Our experienced pediatric team provides compassionate, evidence-based care.

What Is Pediatric Physical Therapy?

Pediatric physical therapy focuses on improving the physical function, strength, mobility, and independence of infants, children, and adolescents. Unlike adult physiotherapy, pediatric treatment uses play-based approaches to engage children while addressing developmental delays, neurological conditions, and musculoskeletal issues.

At Tadawi Clinic, our pediatric physiotherapists work closely with families to create a supportive, fun environment where children feel comfortable while making meaningful progress toward their goals.

Conditions We Treat

  • Developmental Delays: Children who are not meeting motor milestones such as rolling, sitting, crawling, standing, or walking at expected ages
  • Cerebral Palsy: Comprehensive management including tone management, postural control, mobility training, and orthotic prescription for all types and GMFCS levels
  • Torticollis (Head Tilt): Congenital muscular torticollis involving tightness of the sternocleidomastoid muscle, often associated with plagiocephaly
  • Plagiocephaly: Positional head flattening managed through repositioning, tummy time, and specialized helmet therapy referrals
  • Hypotonia (Low Muscle Tone): Generalized or focal low muscle tone affecting posture, strength, and motor development
  • Genetic Conditions: Syndrome-specific physiotherapy for Down syndrome, Prader-Willi, muscular dystrophies, and other genetic conditions
  • Orthopedic Conditions: Clubfoot (post-Ponseti management), hip dysplasia, limb length discrepancies, and juvenile idiopathic arthritis
  • Sports Injuries in Young Athletes: Growth plate injuries, Osgood-Schlatter, Sever's disease, and overuse injuries
  • Respiratory Physiotherapy: Chest physiotherapy for cystic fibrosis, chronic respiratory conditions, and post-surgical recovery
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder: Motor coordination difficulties, sensory processing, and physical activity programs

Our Assessment Process

Our pediatric assessment is thorough, child-friendly, and family-centered:

  • Parent Interview: Detailed history of pregnancy, birth, development, and current concerns
  • Motor Assessment: Standardized tools including the Alberta Infant Motor Scale (AIMS), Peabody Developmental Motor Scales (PDMS), and Gross Motor Function Measure (GMFM)
  • Muscle Tone Assessment: Evaluation of hypertonia, hypotonia, and spasticity using the Modified Ashworth Scale
  • Range of Motion: Joint mobility testing including contracture assessment
  • Strength Testing: Age-appropriate muscle strength assessment
  • Balance and Coordination: Dynamic and static balance evaluation
  • Gait Analysis: Walking pattern assessment for children who are ambulatory
  • Functional Assessment: Evaluation of participation in daily activities and play

Our Treatment Approach

Play-Based Intervention

All treatment sessions are designed around play activities that are motivating and meaningful for the child. We use toys, games, music, and creative activities to achieve therapeutic goals while ensuring the child enjoys the experience.

Neurodevelopmental Techniques

  • Bobath Concept: Facilitating normal movement patterns and inhibiting abnormal tone
  • NDT (Neurodevelopmental Treatment): Hands-on facilitation to improve postural control and movement
  • Vojta Therapy: Reflex locomotion techniques for early intervention
  • Constraint-Induced Movement Therapy (CIMT): For children with hemiplegia to improve affected limb use

Strengthening and Motor Learning

  • Functional Strengthening: Exercises integrated into meaningful activities
  • Task-Specific Training: Repetitive practice of targeted motor skills
  • Balance Training: Progression from static to dynamic balance challenges
  • Aquatic Therapy: Water-based exercises for children who benefit from reduced gravity environment

Family-Centered Care

  • Parent Education: Teaching parents strategies to support development at home
  • Home Program: Age-appropriate activities for daily practice
  • Milestone Guidance: Advice on positioning, play, and activity to encourage development
  • School Collaboration: Working with educators to support physical participation

Equipment and Orthotic Management

  • Recommendation and training for standing frames, walkers, and adaptive equipment
  • Orthotic assessment and training (AFOs, SMOs, hand splints)
  • Wheelchair seating assessment and prescription

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should I bring my child for physiotherapy?

+

Pediatric physiotherapy can benefit children from birth through adolescence. Early intervention (ideally before age 2-3) is particularly effective for developmental delays and neurological conditions, as the developing brain has greater neuroplasticity. However, children of any age can benefit from physiotherapy at any stage.

How long are pediatric therapy sessions?

+

Session duration depends on the child's age, attention span, and tolerance. Sessions typically last 30-60 minutes. Younger children or those with limited attention may have shorter sessions, while older children may participate in longer, more intensive activities.

How can I help my child's development at home?

+

Our therapists will provide a customized home program with specific activities tailored to your child's needs. General tips include: encouraging tummy time for infants, limiting time in restrictive devices (car seats, strollers), providing floor play opportunities, reading and singing to encourage movement, and making physical activity part of daily routines.

Will my child enjoy coming to therapy?

+

Our pediatric physiotherapists are skilled at making therapy fun! Sessions incorporate games, toys, music, and activities that are age-appropriate and motivating. Most children look forward to their therapy sessions. We also work closely with parents to ensure the child feels safe and comfortable.
