Shockwave Therapy in Dubai

Advanced extracorporeal shockwave therapy for chronic tendon and soft tissue conditions. Non-invasive, drug-free treatment that stimulates your body's natural healing response.

What Is Shockwave Therapy?

Extracorporeal Shockwave Therapy (ESWT) is a non-invasive treatment that uses high-energy acoustic waves (shockwaves) to stimulate healing in damaged tissue, reduce pain, and promote tissue regeneration. Originally developed for breaking up kidney stones, shockwave therapy has evolved into a highly effective treatment for chronic musculoskeletal conditions.

At Tadawi Clinic, we use state-of-the-art radial and focused shockwave devices to deliver precise, effective treatment for a wide range of chronic tendon and soft tissue conditions.

How Does Shockwave Therapy Work?

Shockwave therapy works through several biological mechanisms:

  • Neovascularization: Stimulates the formation of new blood vessels, improving blood supply and tissue healing
  • Growth Factor Release: Promotes the release of growth factors and cytokines that initiate tissue repair
  • Collagen Synthesis: Enhances collagen production, strengthening damaged tendons
  • Calcification Breakdown: Helps dissolve calcium deposits in conditions like calcific tendonitis
  • Pain Gate Mechanism: Overstimulates nerve fibers to block pain信号 transmission
  • Substance P Reduction: Reduces levels of Substance P, a neurotransmitter involved in chronic pain
  • Anti-Inflammatory Effect: Modulates the inflammatory response to promote healing without excessive inflammation

Conditions Treated with Shockwave Therapy

  • Plantar Fasciitis: Heel pain caused by inflammation or degeneration of the plantar fascia. One of the most responsive conditions to shockwave therapy
  • Achilles Tendinopathy: Chronic Achilles tendon pain and degeneration, both insertional and mid-portion
  • Lateral Epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow): Chronic tendon degeneration at the lateral elbow
  • Medial Epicondylitis (Golfer's Elbow): Chronic tendon degeneration at the medial elbow
  • Calcific Tendonitis of the Shoulder: Calcium deposits within the rotator cuff tendons causing pain and restricted movement
  • Patellar Tendinopathy: Chronic tendon pain below the kneecap ("jumper's knee")
  • Greater Trochanteric Pain Syndrome: Hip pain associated with gluteal tendon pathology
  • Hamstring Tendinopathy: Chronic hamstring tendon pain at the ischial tuberosity
  • Chronic Muscle Tension: Persistent muscle knots and trigger points
  • Shin Splints (Medial Tibial Stress Syndrome): Chronic lower leg pain from repetitive stress
  • Stress Fractures: Adjunctive treatment for delayed union fractures

Types of Shockwave Therapy

  • Radial Shockwave Therapy (rESWT): Uses a pneumatic generator to produce radial pressure waves that penetrate up to 4-5cm. Ideal for larger muscle groups and superficial tendons. The most common type used in clinical practice.
  • Focused Shockwave Therapy (fESWT): Electromagnetic or electrohydraulic generation produces waves focused at a specific depth (up to 12cm). Greater energy density for deeper conditions like calcific tendonitis and hip pathology.

Your Treatment Session

  • Assessment: Your physiotherapist will assess your condition to confirm suitability for shockwave therapy
  • Gel Application: Ultrasound gel is applied to the treatment area for optimal wave transmission
  • Treatment: The shockwave applicator is moved over the target area, delivering pulses. Treatment typically lasts 5-10 minutes
  • Sensation: You may feel a tapping or pulsing sensation. Some discomfort is normal, especially over painful areas
  • Post-Treatment: Mild soreness may occur for 24-48 hours. Ice can be applied if needed
  • Frequency: Treatments are typically spaced 5-7 days apart, with 3-6 sessions required

Benefits of Shockwave Therapy

  • Non-Invasive: No surgery, no injections, no anesthesia required
  • Drug-Free: Avoids the side effects of long-term medication use
  • Quick Sessions: Treatment takes only 5-10 minutes per session
  • Minimal Downtime: Return to normal activities immediately after treatment
  • High Success Rate: Evidence shows 70-80% improvement in chronic tendon conditions
  • Long-Lasting Results: Healing continues for weeks to months after treatment
  • Cost-Effective: Fewer sessions than traditional conservative treatments for chronic conditions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is shockwave therapy painful?

+

You may feel a tapping or pulsing sensation during treatment, and some discomfort over the painful area is normal. However, the intensity can be adjusted to your comfort level. Most patients describe it as uncomfortable but tolerable. Mild soreness may occur for 24-48 hours after treatment.

How many sessions will I need?

+

Most conditions require 3-6 sessions spaced 5-7 days apart. Some patients notice improvement after the first session, while others experience gradual improvement over the treatment course. Your physiotherapist will assess your response and adjust the treatment plan as needed.

What conditions respond best to shockwave therapy?

+

Shockwave therapy is most effective for chronic tendon conditions that have not responded to other conservative treatments, particularly plantar fasciitis, Achilles tendinopathy, tennis elbow, and calcific tendonitis of the shoulder. Acute injuries are generally better managed with other approaches initially.

Are there any side effects?

+

Side effects are generally mild and temporary, including bruising, swelling, redness, or tenderness at the treatment site. These typically resolve within 2-5 days. Serious complications are extremely rare. Shockwave therapy is contraindicated in pregnancy, over open wounds, and in patients with certain blood disorders or on blood-thinning medications.
